# Butter 2.0 — Product Strategy

## Vision

Butter is the warm, golden VS Code theme for developers who code for hours and want their editor to feel like a place rather than a tool. Every color decision prioritizes comfort over drama. Every feature is opt-in. The whole thing feels handmade.

---

## Positioning Statement

**For developers who spend long hours in their editor and have grown tired of cold, neon, or generic themes, Butter is a warm golden VS Code theme that feels cozy rather than harsh — with an optional companion that makes the editor feel a little less lonely.**

Unlike Dracula (neon fatigue) or generic dark blue themes (no personality), Butter is built on the warmth of golden amber — the color of good light, not server racks.

---

## Target User Personas

### Persona 1: "The Fatigued Draculist" (highest conversion)
- Used Dracula or One Dark for 1-2 years
- Has started noticing eye strain after long sessions
- Describes their ideal theme as "warm" or "easier on the eyes"
- Will search "warm VS Code theme" after a bad session
- Converts when they see Butter's palette screenshot

### Persona 2: "The Gruvbox Graduate"
- Loves Gruvbox's warmth philosophy
- Finds the aesthetic dated or wants semantic token support
- Will respond to "Gruvbox, but modern"
- High retention once acquired — they already believe in warm themes

### Persona 3: "The Setup Poster"
- Shares their VS Code setup on Twitter/Reddit/dev.to
- Values distinctiveness and story — "it has a bear companion" is a tweet
- Brings word-of-mouth installs disproportionate to their numbers

### Persona 4: "The Long-Session Worker"
- Data scientist, technical writer, backend engineer
- Spends 6-10 hours/day in VS Code
- Comfort is the only metric that matters
- Discovers Butter via recommendation, stays because of comfort

---

## Unique Selling Points

1. **Warm golden palette — genuinely unclaimed territory.** Not rose, not blue, not neon. Golden amber, like actual butter.
2. **Built for long sessions, not screenshots.** Every color decision tested against: "does this hurt after 4 hours?"
3. **Three variants for every context.** Dark (evening), Light (daytime), Soft (2am). All cohesive, all warm.
4. **Butter Bear.** An optional status bar companion that reacts to coding activity. Non-intrusive, non-annoying, opt-out. Pure delight.
5. **Complete token coverage.** Not "works for JavaScript." Works for JS/TS, Python, Rust, Go, Ruby, Java, C/C++, CSS, HTML, Markdown, JSON, YAML, SQL — every token defined.
6. **Semantic token support.** Modern VS Code semantic highlighting fully implemented.

---

## Feature Tiers

### Core (always on)
- Three theme variants: Butter Dark, Butter Light, Butter Soft
- Full semantic token highlighting
- Complete terminal color harmony
- Coverage for 10+ languages

### Companion (opt-in, default on)
- Butter Bear in status bar
- Mood states: idle, coding, thinking, resting, celebrating, morning
- Activity detection (typing, saves, diagnostics, time of day)
- Morning greeting on first activation
- Click to see current mood message
- Disable with `butter.companion.enabled: false`

### Commands
- `butter.switchTheme` — quick-pick variant switcher
- `butter.toggleCompanion` — show/hide Bear
- `butter.companionMood` — show current mood notification

## Tone and Aesthetic Direction

**Tone:** Warm, unhurried, a little playful. Not corporate. Not trying too hard. The companion speaks like a friend who is also trying to ship something.

**Aesthetic:** Golden hour, not neon midnight. Think warm lamp light, cream paper, amber tea. The aesthetic is "someone's cozy office" not "a cyberpunk server room."

**Design rules:**
1. No pure black backgrounds
2. No neon saturation
3. Warm tint on every neutral (slightly amber, not cool gray)
4. Comments are quiet, not invisible
5. The cursor is always gold
6. Status bar is gold — brand marker visible at all times

---

## Why Install Butter Over Another Theme?

"It's the only warm-golden theme that's also modern, complete, and has a personality."

Every other warm theme is either aged (Gruvbox), rose-dominant (Rose Pine), or pastel-cool (Catppuccin). Butter is the first theme that puts golden amber first and builds a complete modern extension around it.

## Why Keep Butter?

Three reasons people don't switch away from a theme:
1. **It's comfortable** — warm palette, designed for long sessions
2. **It grows on you** — the Bear companion creates attachment
3. **It works everywhere** — complete token coverage means no surprises in a new language

## What Creates Word of Mouth?

1. **The Bear companion** — "this theme has a bear that reacts when your code has errors" is a story worth telling
2. **The name** — "Butter" is memorable and evocative. "I use Butter" sounds better than "I use One Dark Pro"
3. **The morning greeting** — first activation of the day, the Bear says good morning. That moment gets shared.
4. **The Light variant** — warm light themes are rare and good ones are rarer. The person who discovers Butter Light for daytime tells people.
